## 1. Strategic positioning

### The problem we're solving

The current bsgroup.ch positions BSGroup as a generic Swiss IT consultancy: "Pragmatisch. Professionell. Echt Schweiz!" — a tagline every Swiss B2B firm could claim, with no enemy, no rhythm, and no proof. The site shows zero CTAs, no client logos, no case studies, no thought leadership. For a 30-year-old firm with deep banking and insurance specialism, this severely undersells the actual capability.

### The wedge we're claiming

**Full-stack financial services delivery for Swiss banks and insurers — owned, executed, and finished.**

Not strategy theatre (BearingPoint). Not products-first (ti&m, Adnovum). Not global breadth (Synpulse). Not pure operations (Inventx). The empty slot in the competitive map is *insider firm with deep execution depth and no big-consultancy theater* — the trusted, hands-on delivery specialist that mid-sized Swiss banks and Krankenversicherer call when a project must land.

### The buyer

**Mixed funnel.** The site speaks simultaneously to:
- IT and project leadership at banks, insurers, and Krankenversicherer
- Business / Fachbereich leads sponsoring projects
- Procurement evaluating consultancy panels

This means the home page must route, not pitch. Each service page does the deeper pitching work for one buyer profile.

### The proof we have

- 30+ years on the Swiss financial market
- 4 Schweizer Privatbanken, 3 Kantonalbanken, 6 Krankenversicherer, 5 Versicherer
- Named clients available with permission
- Anonymized case studies with metrics available
- Strong consultant tenure (target: 8+ years average — to confirm)
- 95% Wiederbeauftragungsquote (target — to confirm)

### Strategic moves to make

1. **Own "Delivery"** the way Adnovum owns "Security." None of the five competitors has claimed this lane.
2. **Mid-tier execution lane** — Synpulse and BearingPoint price out of mid-sized projects; ti&m and Adnovum lead with products that may not fit. Open lane for senior consultants who deliver without overhead.
3. **Specific Swissness** — *verbindlich, erfahren, eigentümergeführt, lange dabei.* Not the generic "Swissness" claim every competitor makes.

## 2. The voice system

The voice operates on three tiers. Each tier has a different job and a different deployment surface.

### Tier 1 — Master Claim

One line, used everywhere the brand appears. Must capture the whole firm, work for buyers + talent + partners, scale across all surfaces.

### Tier 2 — Pillar Lines

One per service area. Used on service pages, in service-specific PDFs, in targeted ads, on proposal templates for that service. Pillar lines are tied to service lines, not to moods.

### Tier 3 — Campaign and Section Lines

Rotating, contextual. Used for specific moments: a recruiting campaign, a SYRIUS event booth, a piece of thought leadership, the Über uns page, an anniversary post.

### The three rules that hold the system together

1. **One master, no exceptions.** *Macher für den Schweizer Finanzplatz.* appears on every brand surface — homepage hero, footer, signature, business card, LinkedIn. No team substitutes their favorite line at this level.
2. **Pillar lines are tied to service lines, not to moods.** A buyer reading about Solution Services sees the Solution Services line — and recognizes the same brand voice underneath.
3. **Campaign lines are time-boxed.** Each gets a specific use case and a specific window. Otherwise the system decays into a soup of taglines where nothing stands out.

## 3. Master claim and hero block

### Master claim

> **Macher für den Schweizer Finanzplatz.**

### Hero sub-line

> *Wir bringen Ihre Projekte ins Ziel — pragmatisch, erfahren und verbindlich. Seit über 30 Jahren.*

### Why this works

**The master claim** picks a fight. *Macher* is unusual in Swiss B2B — most consultancies don't dare call themselves that. The word does heavy lifting: it positions BSGroup as builders and finishers, not strategists or presenters. It implicitly attacks the "Folienleger" (slide-stackers) without being rude about it. It also recruits well — *Macher gesucht* writes itself into a job ad headline.

**The sub-line** does three jobs in one sentence:

- *Wir bringen Ihre Projekte ins Ziel* — direct claim, second person, puts the buyer in the sentence
- *pragmatisch, erfahren und verbindlich* — three adjectives doing three different jobs (style, substance, relationship), with no overlap
- *Seit über 30 Jahren* — time-anchor that proves "erfahren" without making the reader work for it

**The triplet rhythm** lands cleanly: *pragmatisch · erfahren · verbindlich* — four syllables, three syllables, four syllables. Symmetric cadence with finality. All three words are plain German, which means a 60-year-old VR-Mitglied at a Kantonalbank and a 28-year-old developer both understand every word instantly.

**Verbindlich** is the most underclaimed trait in Swiss banking IT consulting. None of Adnovum, Synpulse, ti&m, BearingPoint, or Inventx uses it on their homepage. Free positioning lane.

### Where the master claim appears

- Homepage hero (the line is the H1 of the entire site)
- Footer of every page
- E-mail signatures of every employee
- Business cards
- LinkedIn company tagline
- Reception backdrop
- Proposal cover pages
- Conference booth backdrops
- Recruiting page H1 (it works as a recruiting claim too)

### What "Pragmatisch. Professionell. Echt Schweiz." was failing at

- **Claim** — every Swiss firm could say it
- **Differentiation** — no enemy, no antagonist
- **Carry** — three short words in a row, no rhythm, no climax

The new line passes all three tests.

## 4. Pillar lines (per service)

### Project Services

> **Aus Plan wird Produktion.**

For Programm- und Projektleitende in Banken und Versicherungen, who need a critical project to actually land. The line captures the moment when a paper plan becomes a live, running system. Clean three-step rhythm. Direct attack on the "we deliver concepts, not outcomes" competitor profile.

**Anti-claim it positions against:** consulting firms that hand over a glossy report and walk away.

### Solution Services

> **Wir kennen die Plattformen, weil wir sie eingeführt haben.**

For Fachbereichs- und IT-Verantwortliche who are deciding on or struggling with a platform (Avaloq, Finnova, Adcubum SYRIUS, BSI CRM). The line establishes practitioner credibility — we don't read about these systems, we've operated them. Strongly differentiates from generalist consultancies and from product vendors who can only sell their own platform.

**Anti-claim it positions against:** Hersteller-Marketing dressed up as advice.

### Quality Services / Seryous-Testing

> **Wenn's einfach wäre, bräuchten Sie uns nicht.**

For Krankenversicherer (and bank QA leads) who need SYRIUS releases to ship without production incidents. Owns the *Seryous-Testing* productized service — Switzerland's specialist for SYRIUS testing. Slightly cheeky, confident — fits the testing context perfectly because testing exists *because* things aren't simple.

**Anti-claim it positions against:** anyone pretending releases are predictable.

### Software Services

> **Wir können nicht alles. Aber Banken und Versicherungen können wir.**

For IT leads needing custom software, modernization, or cloud migration. The line uses self-deprecation as a strength — most consultancies claim breadth; we claim depth. Counters Big-Four breadth and offshore scale with deliberate specialization.

**Anti-claim it positions against:** "we do everything" generalists and offshore body shops.

## 5. Über uns voice

### Headline

> **Wer bei uns anfängt, bleibt.**

### Body paragraph

> *Bei uns bleiben Beraterinnen und Berater im Schnitt über 8 Jahre. Und unsere Kundinnen und Kunden bleiben auch — neun von zehn beauftragen uns wieder. Die Person, die heute Ihr Projekt aufsetzt, bringt es auch zu Ende. Und wahrscheinlich auch das nächste.*

### CTAs

> [Über uns →] [Karriere bei BSGroup →]

### Why this works

**The line carries a double meaning** that the body paragraph reveals: *consultants stay* (8+ years tenure) AND *clients stay* (95% Wiederbeauftragungsquote, expressed as "neun von zehn"). The buyer reads the headline at face value, then has a small moment of recognition in the paragraph — *wait, this is true on both sides.* That's more memorable than announcing the duality upfront.

**"Und wahrscheinlich auch das nächste"** is the closing beat that turns consultant tenure and client retention into the same thing — the *person* stays, so the *relationship* compounds. Implicitly invites the buyer to think "this isn't a one-project decision; this is a multi-year relationship choice."

**The hard number lives in the body**, not the headline. "Neun von zehn beauftragen uns wieder" is a natural-language version of the 95% KPI from the proof bar earlier on the page. Same fact, two different registers — that's how a number sticks.

### Confirmation needed

- Average consultant tenure: confirm "über 8 Jahre" with HR before launch
- Client repeat rate: confirm "neun von zehn" / "95%" with sales before launch
- Both numbers live on the home page KPI strip and in this paragraph — they must match exactly

## 8. Page-by-page content overview

### Home

The orchestrator page. Doesn't try to convert directly — sends qualified buyers to the right service page. Ten sections from top to bottom: Hero, Proof bar, Why BSGroup, Service teasers (4 blocks), Featured cases (3 tiles), KPI strip (4 numbers), Über uns teaser, Insights teaser (3 articles), Final CTA, Footer.

### Project Services

Hero with "Aus Plan wird Produktion." → The problem we solve → What we do (project leadership, PMO, programme management) → Typical mandates (Kernbanken-Migrationen, Regulatorik FINMA/FIDLEG/AIA/Basel IV, Plattform-Konsolidierungen, Self-Onboarding-Programme) → Method (Discovery → Setup → Steuerung → Übergabe) → One full case study with numbers → FAQ for procurement → CTA.

### Solution Services

Hero with "Wir kennen die Plattformen, weil wir sie eingeführt haben." → Why platform decisions matter → Platform expertise (4 blocks: Avaloq, Finnova, Adcubum SYRIUS, BSI CRM) → Vendor-neutral evaluation method (the "we have no reseller incentive" angle) → Reference case → CTA.

The single highest-distinctiveness page on the site. Should arguably be its own micro-site. Hero with "Wenn's einfach wäre, bräuchten Sie uns nicht." → The Seryous-Testing service (Testmanagement, Testdurchführung, Testautomatisierung) → What you get → Reference case (18→6 day regression cycle) → Why us vs alternatives (in-house vs offshore vs BSGroup) → Method → Tooling (Tosca, qTest, Jira/Xray, etc.) → CTA + downloadable Service Brief for SEO.

### Software Services

Hero with "Wir können nicht alles. Aber Banken und Versicherungen können wir." → When custom beats standard → Engineering focus (Custom Software Engineering, ALM, Software-/Datenmigrationen, Cloud Re-Engineering, Dokumenten-/Datenmanagement) → Tech stack (Java, .NET, frontend frameworks, Azure/AWS, Kafka, Docker/Kubernetes) → Reference case → CTA.

Hero with "Was wir geliefert haben." + filter bar → Case study grid (9–12 tiles, 3-column) → Logo wall (named where allowed + anonymized count strip below) → Testimonials (3–5 short ones) → KPI strip → CTA: "Eine Referenz wie Ihre dabei?" with the killer line "inklusive der Sachen, die schiefgegangen sind und wie wir sie gerichtet haben."

Hero with "Klartext für komplexe Projekte." → Featured article → Article grid (6–10 articles) → Filter by topic → Newsletter signup ("Quartalsweise. Kein Spam. Nur Klartext.") → Whitepaper / downloads.

Hero with "Wer bei uns anfängt, bleibt." → History (3 paragraphs, one per decade) → Was uns ausmacht (4–5 short paragraphs on culture) → KPI strip → Leadership team (photos + bios + LinkedIn) → Office (real photos, not stock) → CTA.

Hero with "Macher gesucht." + sub-line about average tenure → Why BSGroup (3 concrete reasons: project ownership, no churn, real responsibility) → Open roles (filterable) → Berater-Stimmen (2–3 quotes from real consultants) → Process (timeline, expectations) → CTA.

Hero with "Reden wir über Ihr Vorhaben." → Two paths: Konkrete Anfrage (4-field form: Name, E-Mail, Unternehmen, Worum geht's?) and Erste Sondierung (calendar booking with 2 named senior consultants, photos, brief bios) → Contact details → Standort (map + photo).

> *"BSGroup hat unsere Avaloq-Migration übernommen, nachdem ein anderer Anbieter sechs Monate gebraucht hat, um nicht voranzukommen. Wir waren in 14 Wochen produktiv."*
> — IT-Leiter, Schweizer Privatbank

### Section 5 — KPI strip

> **200+** abgeschlossene Projekte · **95%** Wiederbeauftragungsquote · **0** öffentlich bekannte Projektabbrüche in 30 Jahren · **30+** Jahre am Schweizer Finanzplatz

The "0 öffentlich bekannte Projektabbrüche" is the killer number if it's defensible. *"Never publicly walked away from a project"* is something almost no consultancy can claim.

### Section 6 — CTA

> ## Eine Referenz wie Ihre dabei?
> *Senior-Beraterin oder Senior-Berater zeigt Ihnen in 30 Minuten zwei bis drei vergleichbare Mandate im Detail — inklusive der Sachen, die schiefgegangen sind und wie wir sie gerichtet haben.*
>
> [Termin buchen →] [Vollständige Referenzliste anfragen (NDA) →]

The "inklusive der Sachen, die schiefgegangen sind" line is doing the most work. Signals BSGroup isn't selling perfection; it's selling people who've actually done this and are honest about the texture of the work. Massive trust-builder.

### Detail page structure

Each tile clicks through to its own page (~600–900 words, 3-min read):
1. Title block — Industry + project type + headline metric
2. Ausgangslage (3–4 sentences)
3. Auftrag (2–3 sentences)
4. Vorgehen — 3–5 short paragraphs, names methods/tools/platforms
5. Ergebnis — outcomes with numbers
6. Was wir daraus gelernt haben — one paragraph of reflection (the differentiator)
7. Beratungsteam — names, roles, photos, LinkedIn of 2–4 actual consultants
8. Inline CTA back to relevant service page

### Three rules for keeping the page sharp

1. Every tile must have a number. No exceptions.
2. Cap the grid at 12 tiles, ever. Beyond that, scannability collapses.
3. Refresh quarterly. Rotate one tile per quarter.

## 12. Voice rules

### What the BSGroup voice is

- **Direct** — short sentences. Active voice. Verbs do the work.
- **Plain German** — no compound jargon, no Anglicisms unless technically required.
- **Specific** — names platforms, names numbers, names roles.
- **Confident, not loud** — Swiss-coded restraint. Never exclamation marks.
- **Slightly contrarian** — willing to make claims that have an enemy.

### What the BSGroup voice is not

- **Not aspirational** — no "transformation journeys," no "unleashing potential," no "unlocking value."
- **Not hedged** — no "we believe," no "we strive to," no "we aim to be."
- **Not generic** — no "innovation," no "excellence," no "passion" as standalone claims.
- **Not corporate purpose-language** — no "people, planet, innovation."
- **Not playful at the master level** — Karriere can be lighter; the brand voice is steady.

### Words to avoid

- *Synergie, Transformation, Journey, Customer Experience, Excellence, Passion, Empowerment*
- *Wir glauben, wir streben, wir bemühen uns, wir möchten*
- *Innovativ, dynamisch, ganzheitlich, wegweisend*

### Words to use

- *Liefern, umsetzen, einführen, parametrieren, dokumentieren, begleiten, abschliessen*
- *Pragmatisch, erfahren, verbindlich, konsequent, unaufgeregt*
- *Avaloq, Finnova, SYRIUS, BSI CRM, FINMA, FIDLEG, AIA, Basel IV* (specifics are credibility)

### Number rules

- Always concrete. "47%" not "approximately 50%."
- Always with context. "280'000 Kundenstämme migriert" not "viele Kunden migriert."
- Hard numbers in body copy, percentages in KPI strips.

### Punctuation and formatting

- **Em dashes (—)** instead of parentheses where possible. They feel more decisive.
- **Single sentences over bullet points** in prose sections.
- **Bullet points only when the list itself is the content** (technologies, mandates, options).
- **Sentence case** for headlines. Never Title Case, never ALL CAPS.

### What gets first-person ("wir")

The whole site is in *wir*-form. BSGroup speaks as the firm, not as an anonymous brand. Senior-Beratende names appear in case studies, on the booking page, in testimonials. The firm has people, and the website shows them.
